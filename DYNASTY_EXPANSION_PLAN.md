# Dynasty Expansion Plan

## Current Status

**Tracked:** 31 dynasties  
**Reality:** ~250 political families in Philippines  
**Coverage:** 12% of total political dynasties

## The Gap

We're currently tracking only the most high-profile dynasties:
- National power dynasties (Marcos, Duterte, Aquino, etc.)
- Major regional dynasties with national connections
- Dynasties with significant 2025 events or corruption scandals

**Missing:** The vast majority of regional and local dynasties that control provinces and cities.

## Research Methodology

### Sources
1. **PCIJ (Philippine Center for Investigative Journalism)**
   - Dynasty reports and research
   - Election analysis

2. **Election Results**
   - 2025 midterm election results by province
   - Historical election data
   - Identify families with multiple members in office

3. **Local News Sources**
   - Provincial newspapers
   - Regional news outlets
   - Local political coverage

4. **Academic Research**
   - Studies on political dynasties
   - Governance research
   - Political science papers

5. **Government Records**
   - COMELEC election results
   - Provincial government records
   - Congressional records

### Data Collection Process

For each dynasty:
1. **Identify the family** - Name, patriarch/matriarch
2. **Geographic control** - Which provinces/cities
3. **Current positions** - Who holds what office
4. **Historical span** - When established, peak power
5. **Key members** - Current political figures
6. **Relationships** - Alliances, rivalries, marriages
7. **2025 events** - Any significant events
8. **Corruption connections** - Any allegations or cases

### Validation

- Cross-reference multiple sources
- Verify current positions (2025-2026)
- Check for recent changes (elections, resignations)
- Validate relationships with other dynasties
